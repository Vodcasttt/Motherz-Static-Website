Munnar

The southern state of Kerala is blessed with abundant beauty, lush greenery, cultural heritage, and vast tea gardens, and is rightfully called â€˜Gods' Own Country'. Within the state, the hill station of Munnar located in the Idukki district deserves a special mention. The hill station is popular for trekking trails, sightseeing, sprawling tea gardens, natural bliss, and delicious Kerala cuisine, and is one of the most visited tourist destinations in Kerala.

Echo Point

One of the most popular tourist attractions in Munnar, the Echo Point lets you trek, walk, stroll or indulge in adventure activities around its meandering path. An interesting thing here is that if you scream your name out loud at this point, it will echo within 3 seconds!

Eravikulam National Park

The Eravikulam National Park houses one of the largest populations of the endangered Nilgiri tahr and is home to a varity of flora and fauna, including 26 different species of mammals. It is counted among the other UNESCO World Heritage sites. The park also offers a stunning view of the adjoining Western Ghats.

Mattupetty Dam

One of the famous picnic spots in Munnar is Mattupetty Dam. The dam is built on the Mattupetty Lake which is home to several species of birds and animals due to the perennial supply of water through the reservoir. One can enjoy a nice stroll by the lake or can try boating.
